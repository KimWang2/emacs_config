Judge Clears 4 Men Wrongly Accused of 1991 Austin Yogurt Shop Murders
The men were formally declared innocent after new evidence linked the killings to a suspect who died in 1999.

A Texas judge has formally declared four men innocent of the 1991 Austin yogurt shop murders, closing a dark chapter in the city's history. The four men - Michael Scott, Forrest Welborn, Robert Springsteen, and Maurice Pierce - were wrongfully accused of the brutal killings of four teenage girls. After decades of investigations and false confessions, new DNA evidence has linked the murders to a suspect who died in a 1999 standoff with police.

Why it matters
The declaration of innocence is an important step for the men and their families, who lived under the specter of the unsolved murders for over 25 years. It also highlights the fallibility of the criminal justice system and the need for rigorous evidence standards, especially in high-profile cases. The case shook the Austin community and its inability to solve the crime for decades.

The details
In 1991, four teenage girls - Amy Ayers, 13; Eliza Thomas, 17; and sisters Jennifer and Sarah Harbison, 17 and 15 - were bound, gagged, and shot at the 'I Can't Believe It's Yogurt' shop where two of them worked. The building was then set on fire. Investigators chased thousands of leads and false confessions before arresting the four men in 1999. Springsteen and Scott were convicted based on coerced confessions, but their convictions were later overturned. Welborn was charged but never tried, while Pierce spent three years in jail before being released. In 2009, new DNA tests led a judge to dismiss the charges against Springsteen and Scott. It wasn't until 2025 that investigators linked the murders to Robert Eugene Brashers, who died in a 1999 standoff with police.
